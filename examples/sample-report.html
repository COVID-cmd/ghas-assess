Push protection not bypassed
Now: 2 push-protection bypass(es) recorded - each pushed a detected secret past the block.
Best practice: No secrets pushed via a push-protection bypass; any bypass reviewed.
Why it matters: A push-protection bypass means a developer deliberately pushed a detected secret past the block. Each one is a potential live credential in git history that must be reviewed and rotated.
Why flagged (evidence): bypasses = 2
What to do: Review every bypass; rotate any exposed secret; require justification for bypasses.
SECRET-PUSH-PROTECTION GAP High repo:payments-service
Push protection enabled
Now: Secret scanning enabled but PUSH PROTECTION DISABLED - secrets detected after commit.
Best practice: Push protection ON, so secrets are blocked before they enter git history.
Why it matters: Without push protection, a leaked credential is only detected AFTER it is committed - by then it must be treated as compromised and rotated. Blocking at push time prevents the exposure entirely.
Why flagged (evidence): push_protection = disabled
What to do: Enable push protection (ideally enforced via the org security configuration so it cannot be turned off per repo).
SECRET-AI-DETECTION GAP Medium repo:payments-service
AI-detected secrets enabled
Now: AI-detected secrets not enabled - non-conforming credentials may escape detection.
Best practice: AI/non-provider secret detection ON, in addition to pattern matching.
Why it matters: Regex patterns only catch credentials with a known shape. Hardcoded passwords, connection strings and home-grown tokens have no fixed format and slip through - AI detection covers that residual class.
What to do: Enable AI-detected secrets; measure detection quality on a pilot repo first.
SECRET-CUSTOM-PATTERNS GAP Medium org
Custom secret patterns defined
Now: No organisation custom secret-scanning patterns defined.
Best practice: Custom patterns defined for organisation-specific credential formats.
Why it matters: Built-in patterns cover public cloud/SaaS tokens. Internal API keys and service credentials unique to your organisation are invisible to them and leak silently.
Why flagged (evidence): custom_patterns = 0
What to do: Define custom patterns (dry-run first, then enforce) for your internal formats.
